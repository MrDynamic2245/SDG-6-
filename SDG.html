SDG 6: Clean Water & Sanitation

Every year in the world, 3.6 billion people do not have access to water, 5 million people die from dehydration, and 1.8 billion homes still do not have safe water to drink. And what I'm going to talk about is not a new problem anymore. It has been a problem that has been affecting us globally since the 1800s. That is more than 200 years of not having clean water to drink. This presentation will show you how to fix this living nightmare that has been affecting half of the world's population for two centuries.


The significance of Clean Water & Sanitation

Ensure access to water and sanitation for all. Access to safe water, sanitation and hygiene is the most basic human need for health and well-being.

Targets of Clean Water

6.1 - Safe and affordable drinking water.
6.2 - End open defecation and provide access to sanitation and hygiene.
6.3 - Improve water quality wastewater treatment and safe reuse.
6.4 - Increase water-use effeciency and ensure freshwater supplies.
6.5 - Implemented intergrated water resources management.
6.6 - Protect and restore water related ecosystems.
6.7 - Expand water sanitation support to developing countries.
6.8 - Support local engagement in water and sanitation management.
